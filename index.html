The greatest lie ever told
Wednesday, April 27, 2010.

The greatest lie ever told is that some people are predisposed to things, and some aren't.

I took to coding and math really fast as a kid. My sisters were more creative types.

It's not true.

It took me a long time to figure this out, essentially because the belief that I was just a technical person was engrained so hard in me that I never bothered to think otherwise.

The amount of skill that you have in a certain area is proportional to the amount of work that you put into it. There is no such thing is a 'creative' or 'technical' type. To be clear, I don't deny that people may enjoy one subject more than another, which could have a confounding effect.
