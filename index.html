The greatest lie ever told
Wednesday, April 27, 2010.

The greatest lie ever told is that some people are predisposed to things, and some aren't.

I took to coding and math really fast as a kid. My sisters were more creative types. I would try to make art for my games, but I just couldn't make it look good. My parents would see my attempts and nod. "You're not an artistic type," they would say.

It's not true.

It took me a long time to figure this out. The belief that I was 'just a technical person' was so strong that I didn't realize I could think otherwise.

The amount of skill that you have in a certain area is proportional to the amount of work that you put into it. There is no such thing as a 'creative' or 'technical' type. The reason I was bad at art starting out is because we are bad at anything starting out. It's as simple as that. To be clear, I don't deny that people may enjoy one subject more than another, which could have a confounding effect.
