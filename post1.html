The greatest lie ever told
Wednesday, April 27, 2010.

The greatest lie ever told is that we have innate abities.

I took to math and coding - especially games - as a kid. But when I tried to make art for my games, it was awful. My parents would see my scrawling and nod. "You're not an artistic type," they would say.

We split the world into dichotomies. Fuzzies and techies. Creative and technical people. It's common knowledge that crossing from the side you're on to the other is hopeless.

This isn't true.

It took me a long time to figure this out. The belief that I was 'just a technical person' was so strong that I didn't realize I could step outside of that label. I didn't understand it was a label. I thought it was truth.

The amount of skill that you have in a certain area is proportional to the amount of work that you put into it. There is no such thing as a 'creative' or 'technical' type. The reason I was bad at art starting out is the same reason we are bad at anything starting out. As soon as I started putting hours of practice, I got better. To be clear, I don't deny that people may enjoy one subject more than another, which could have a confounding effect.

This realization was life changing. I can be good at anything that I dedicate myself to.

What restrictions have others placed on you?
